I would like to use this blog for two main reasons:
* improve my english;
* delve deeply into topics I find interesting.

## English

While I'm working I feel that I don't have any problem to explain my ideas, saying exactly what I want to say, without flights of fancy.

The problems come in the common speech with friends outside work, where I should "distill" what I want to say, because the idea that I have in mind it's too complex for my poor English skills. It's like using a car where the engine power is bottlenecked by aerodynamic inefficiency of the chassis (I swear that it's not a metaphor from a LLM).

There are two main reasons for that:
* poverty of my non-technical vocabulary;
* ignorance about grammar.

### Vocabulary

The small size of the vocabulary is reasonable: when I was in high school I never studied English and my knowledge comes only from YouTube videos and university books, both of which are things about Computer Science. The problem it's not only the words that I miss but in particular the lack of knowledge of idioms and phrasal verbs, that are rarely used in technical English, but make your exposure more enjoyable.

### Grammar

Even if the vocabulary is improved by osmosis, watching a lot of videos, I can't say the same for the grammar. The most critical problem is with verb tenses, I always use them as they come naturally, without having any idea if the tense that I'm using exists or if it's the right situation for using it.

To address these problems I will:
* diversify my inputs, watching and reading stuff in English, even if it's non-technical;
* study grammar, with the help of a lot of exercises.

In this blog I will practice what I learnt.

## Deep Dive

There are a lot of topics that I studied during the university and that I found interesting, but that I barely rememeber and that probably I have never understood.

The problem with university exams is that, if you want to maximize the grade and minimize the time spent, you should learn how to do the exam, not the subject of the exam. Take all the past exams, do all of them, find patterns and memorize them.
