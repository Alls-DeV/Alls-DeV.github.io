## Why a blog?

I would like to use this blog for two main reasons:

* Improve my English;
* Delve deeply into topics I find interesting.

While I'm working I feel that I don't have any problem explaining my ideas, saying exactly what I want to say, without flights of fancy.

The problems come in casual conversation with friends outside work, where I have to "distill" what I want to say, because the idea that I have in mind is too complex for my poor English skills. It's like using a car where the engine power is bottlenecked by the aerodynamic inefficiency of the chassis (I swear that it's not a metaphor from an LLM).

There are two main reasons for that:
* Limited non-technical vocabulary;
* Lack of grammar knowledge.

### Vocabulary

The small size of the vocabulary is reasonable: when I was in high school I never studied English and my knowledge comes only from YouTube videos and university books, both of which are things about Computer Science. The problem is not only the words that I miss but in particular the lack of knowledge of idioms and phrasal verbs, that are rarely used in technical English, but make your speech more enjoyable.

### Grammar

Even though the vocabulary is improved by osmosis, watching a lot of videos, I can't say the same for the grammar. The most critical problem is with verb tenses: I always use them as they come naturally, without having any idea if the tense that I'm using exists or if it's the right situation for using it.

To address these problems I will:
* Diversify my inputs, watching and reading stuff in English, even if it's non-technical;
* Study grammar, with the help of a lot of exercises.

In this blog I will practice what I learnt, writing about what is interesting for me. In this way I can use a sort of Feynman technique, to check if I truly understood what I studied.
